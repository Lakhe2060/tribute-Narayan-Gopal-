Narayan Gopal

One of the most celebrated and influential musicians of the 20th century.

Here's a timeline of Narayan Gopal's life
  • 1939 - Born on October 4 in Kilagal, Kathmandu,Nepal, into a Brahmin family. Developed a passion for music despite family opposition.
  • 1954 - Records his first song, marking his entry into the Nepali music scene.
  • 1960 - Gains recognition with songs featuring lyrics by famous poets like Bhupi Sherchan and Laxmi Prasad Devkota.
  • 1960s - Establishes himself as the "Tragedy King" of Nepali music through his soulful and emotive singing.
  • 1970 - Releases iconic songs like Euta Manchhe Ko Maya Le Kati and Yo Samjhine Man Cha, earning nationwide fame.
  • 1980 - Becomes widely recognized as Nepal's greatest singer, receiving honors such as the Indra Rajya Laxmi Award.
  • 1985 - Performs in various live concerts, cementing his legacy as a beloved performer and cultural icon.
  • 1990 - Passes away on December 5 at the age of 51 due to complications from diabetes.
  • 1991 - Posthumously celebrated for his contributions to Nepali music; his works are remembered as cultural treasures.
  • 2020s - Annual tributes and music festivals honor his legacy, keeping his timeless songs alive in the hearts of fans.
